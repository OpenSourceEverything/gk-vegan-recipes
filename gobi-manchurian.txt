--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Gobi Manchurian (Baked Cauliflower, Batter + Sauce)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Cal: ~720 kcal (per batch)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ingredients:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- For the cauliflower:
  - 1 small head of cauliflower, cut into bite-sized florets

- For the batter:
  - 1/2 tablespoons all-purpose flour or rice flour
  - 1/4 cup cornstarch or tapioca starch
  - 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  - 1/4 teaspoon salt
  - 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  - 2 teaspoons oil
  - 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  - 1/3 to 1/2 cup water
  - 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs

- For the sauce:
  - 2 teaspoons oil

  - BOWL 1:
    - 6-8 cloves garlic, finely chopped
    - 1-inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
    - 1/2 green or red chili, seeded and finely chopped
    - 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
    - 1/2 cup finely chopped green bell pepper
    - 2 tablespoons chopped celery (optional)

  - BOWL 2:
    - 1 tablespoon Asian chili sauce (e.g., Sambal Oelek)
    - 1/4 cup soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon ketchup
    - 2 teaspoons white or apple cider vinegar
    - 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
    - 2-3 teaspoons sugar or maple syrup

  - 1 1/2 teaspoons cornstarch
  - 1/2 cup water

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Instructions:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Prepare the Batter:
   - In a bowl, combine flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, salt, and baking powder.
   - Add soy sauce, oil, and 1/3 cup water; mix until smooth.
   - Adjust water as needed to achieve a thick, smooth batter.

2. Coat the Cauliflower:
   - Add cauliflower florets to the batter; toss to coat evenly.
   - Sprinkle panko breadcrumbs over the coated florets for extra crispiness.

3. Bake the Cauliflower:
   - Preheat oven to 415°F (210°C).
   - Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
   - Arrange coated florets on the sheet in a single layer.
   - Bake for 25-28 minutes, checking for doneness at 25 minutes.

4. Prepare the Sauce:
   - Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
   - Sauté garlic, ginger, and chili until fragrant (2-3 minutes).
   - Add onion and bell pepper; cook until softened.
   - Stir in chili sauce, soy sauce, ketchup, vinegar, black pepper, and sugar; bring to a boil.
   - Mix cornstarch with 1/2 cup water to create a slurry; add to the skillet.
   - Cook until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy.

5. Combine and Serve:
   - Add baked cauliflower to the sauce; toss to coat evenly.
   - Serve immediately, garnished with chopped green onions if desired.
