--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Miso Soup (Dashi + Veg + Tofu, 2 Servings)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Base: 1 L dashi (kombu + shiitake)
  - Notes: add miso off-heat to preserve flavor.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ingredients:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Dashi (prepared)         - 1 L
- Miso Paste (refrigerated)- 2-3 tbsp (to taste)
- Tofu (firm or soft)      - 200-250g, cubed
- Bok Choy                 - 2 small heads (separate leaves; stems 1/4 in, leaves 1 in)
- Green Cabbage            - 1 cup, thinly sliced
- Carrot                   - 1 small (matchsticks or thin half-moons, 1.5-2 in)
- Spinach                  - 2 cups (stack, roll, slice 1/2 in ribbons)
- Green Onion              - 2-3 stalks (light bias, 1/8-1/4 in)
- Fresh Shiitake           - 4-6 (remove stems, 1/8-1/4 in slices)
- Dried Shiitake           - 2-3 (soak warm 20-30 min; slice; save liquid)
- Wakame (dried)           - 1 tbsp (soaked)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Instructions:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Soak wakame in cold water 5 minutes; drain. Soak dried shiitake in warm water
   20-30 minutes, squeeze, slice, and strain liquid into the dashi.
2. Bring dashi to a gentle simmer. Add carrots, cabbage, and bok choy stems.
3. Simmer 4-6 minutes until just tender.
4. Add fresh shiitake, dried shiitake, and tofu. Simmer 2-3 minutes.
5. Add bok choy leaves; cook 1-2 minutes.
6. Remove from heat. Add spinach to wilt.
7. Dissolve miso in a ladle of hot broth, then stir into pot.
8. Add wakame and green onion (end or garnish). Taste and adjust with more miso.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Notes:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Do not boil after adding miso or it can taste harsh.
- If your dashi is very light, use closer to 3 tbsp miso.
- General: thin cuts, spoon-size bites.
