--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Korean-Style Roasted Chickpeas (2 Flavor Options)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Cal: ~1000 per batch (makes 3-4 servings)
  - Protein: ~25g per batch
  - Prep: 10 min | Roast: 25-30 min | Total: 40 min
  - Storage: 3 days airtight container (re-crisp in oven if needed)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Base Ingredients (Both Flavors):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Dried chickpeas             - 275g (2 cups) dry → ~660g (3 cups) cooked
  OR Canned chickpeas         - 1¾ cans (15oz each), drained & rinsed
- Neutral oil                 - 2 Tbsp
- Salt                        - 1 tsp (for initial roasting)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FLAVOR 1: KOREAN GOCHUJANG-SOY (Classic)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sauce Mix:
- Gochujang                   - 2½ Tbsp (~50g)
- Soy sauce                   - 2 Tbsp
- Maple syrup                 - 2 Tbsp (or honey)
- Garlic powder               - 2 tsp
- Sesame oil                  - 2 tsp
- Rice vinegar                - 1 tsp

Garnish:
- Sesame seeds                - 2 tsp (toasted)
- Green onions (optional)     - 2 Tbsp chopped

Calories: ~1410 total | Sodium: ~2000mg | Sweet-spicy-umami profile

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FLAVOR 2: YANGNYEOM-STYLE (Korean Sweet & Sticky)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sauce Mix:
- Gochujang                   - 2 Tbsp
- Ketchup                     - 2 Tbsp
- Soy sauce                   - 1 Tbsp
- Maple syrup                 - 2 Tbsp
- Garlic powder               - 2 tsp
- Onion powder                - 1 tsp
- Ginger powder               - ½ tsp

Garnish:
- Sesame seeds                - 2 tsp
- Nori flakes (optional)      - 1 tsp

Calories: ~1380 total | Sodium: ~1600mg | Sweet-tangy-sticky profile

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Instructions:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1: PREP CHICKPEAS
1. If using dried: Soak 275g (2 cups) overnight, then cook until tender
2. If using canned: Drain & rinse 1¾ cans thoroughly
3. Pat completely dry with paper towels (CRITICAL for crispiness)
4. Optional: Remove loose skins for extra crispiness
5. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C)

STEP 2: INITIAL ROAST
1. Toss dried chickpeas with 2 Tbsp oil and 1 tsp salt
2. Spread on large baking sheet in single layer (use 2 pans if needed)
3. Roast 25-30 minutes, shaking pan every 10 minutes
4. Chickpeas should be golden and crispy when done

STEP 3: MAKE SAUCE
1. While chickpeas roast, whisk all sauce ingredients in small bowl
2. Taste and adjust: more gochujang for heat, maple syrup for sweetness

STEP 4: GLAZE & FINISH
1. Remove hot chickpeas from oven
2. Immediately toss with sauce mixture while still hot
3. Return to oven 3-5 minutes to set glaze
4. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and garnishes
5. Cool slightly before eating (they'll get crispier as they cool)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tips for Maximum Crispiness:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- MUST be completely dry before roasting
- Don't add sauce until after initial roasting
- Single layer only - use multiple pans if needed
- Shake pan every 10 minutes during roasting
- Let cool 5 minutes after glazing for best texture

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Flavor Variations:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EXTRA HEAT: Add ¼ tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes to sauce
SMOKY: Add ½ tsp smoked paprika to dry seasoning
CITRUS: Add ½ tsp lime zest to finished chickpeas
UMAMI BOOST: Add 1 tsp nutritional yeast to sauce

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Nutrition Breakdown (Flavor 1 - Gochujang-Soy):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Chickpeas (275g dry)           → ~1000 cal, ~25g protein
- Oil (2 Tbsp)                   → ~240 cal, 0g protein
- Gochujang (50g)                → ~60 cal, ~3g protein
- Maple syrup (2 Tbsp)           → ~100 cal, 0g protein
- Other seasonings (~15g)        → ~10 cal, ~1g protein
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Total (Flavor 1)                → ~1410 cal, ~29g protein
Per ¼ batch serving              → ~350 cal, ~7g protein

Note: Chickpeas provide base 1000 cal target, sauce adds additional calories

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Chickpea Conversions:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- 275g dry chickpeas = 2 cups dry = ~660g cooked = ~3 cups cooked
- 1¾ cans (15oz each) = ~740g drained = equivalent to 275g dry
- Dry chickpeas: 3.64 cal/g, soak overnight then cook 45-60 min

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Storage & Serving:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Serve immediately for best crispiness
- Store leftovers airtight up to 3 days
- Re-crisp in 350°F oven for 5 minutes if needed
- Great snack, salad topper, or rice bowl addition
- Pairs well with: Korean rice bowls, ramen, Asian salads
